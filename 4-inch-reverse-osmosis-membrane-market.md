Composite membranes are made up of multiple layers, each with specific functions such as rejection of contaminants and high water permeability. Asymmetric membranes have a dense active layer on top of a porous support layer, providing better filtration capabilities. Both types of membranes are used in reverse osmosis systems for efficient water purification in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, food and beverage, and water treatment.</p></p>
