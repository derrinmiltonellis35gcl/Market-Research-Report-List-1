<p><p>Rubber vulcanizers are categorized into different types based on their chemical composition and reactivity. Elemental sulfur vulcanizers are commonly used as a traditional curing agent for rubber, creating strong cross-links between polymer chains. Peroxide vulcanizing agents are preferred for heat-resistant rubber compounds, as they initiate cross-linking through a radical mechanism. Quinone derivatives are another type of vulcanizer that provide good heat resistance and aging properties to rubber products. Other types of vulcanizers may include different chemical compounds or blends tailored for specific rubber applications.</p></p>
