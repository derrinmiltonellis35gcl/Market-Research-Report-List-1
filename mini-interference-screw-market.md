<p><p>A mini interference screw is a medical device used in orthopedic surgery to fixate ligaments and tendons in the bone. The screw is made of bioabsorbable material, usually polymer or metal, and is inserted into the bone to secure the ligament or tendon in place.</p><p>The mini interference screw market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years. One trend is the increasing preference for bioabsorbable screws over metallic screws due to their biocompatibility and ability to reduce the risk of complications such as implant-related infections. Manufacturers are focusing on producing screws with improved bone-to-screw purchase and better fixation strength.</p><p>Moreover, the market is witnessing a surge in research and development activities aimed at introducing novel materials and technologies to enhance the performance and biocompatibility of mini interference screws.
