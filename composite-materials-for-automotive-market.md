<p><p>Composite materials for the automotive market are becoming increasingly popular due to their lightweight and high-strength properties. Three common types include Sheet Molding Compound (SMC), Fiber Reinforced Plastic (FRP), and Resin Transfer Molding (RTM). SMC is a compression molding process that combines fiberglass reinforcement with thermoset resin. FRP is a versatile material made from a polymer matrix reinforced with fibers such as glass or carbon. RTM involves injecting resin into a closed mold cavity containing reinforcing fibers. These materials offer enhanced performance and fuel efficiency for automotive applications.</p></p>
