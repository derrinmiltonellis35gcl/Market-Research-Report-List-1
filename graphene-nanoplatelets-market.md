<p><p>Graphene Nanoplatelets (GNPs) are classified into two main types: Multilayer Graphene Nanoplatelets (M-GNPs) and Few-layer Graphene Nanoplatelets (C-GNPs). M-GNPs are made up of multiple layers of graphene sheets stacked on top of each other, offering high strength and conductivity. On the other hand, C-GNPs consist of only a few layers of graphene, providing enhanced flexibility and surface area. Both types have unique properties that make them popular in various industries such as electronics, aerospace, and energy storage.</p></p>
