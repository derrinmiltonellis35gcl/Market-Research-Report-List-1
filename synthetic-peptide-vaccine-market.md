<p><p>Synthetic peptide vaccines are classified into Solution Phase Synthesis (SPS) and Solid Phase Peptide Synthesis (SPPS) markets. SPS involves synthesizing peptides in solution, allowing for faster and simpler production. SPPS, on the other hand, involves synthesizing peptides on a solid support, enabling more efficient purification and higher-quality peptides. Both methods are widely used in the development of synthetic peptide vaccines, each offering unique advantages in terms of scalability, cost-effectiveness, and quality of the final product.</p></p>
