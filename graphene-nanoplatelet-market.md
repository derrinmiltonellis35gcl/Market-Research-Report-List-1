<p><p>Graphene Nanoplatelets can be classified into Hydrophobic and Hydrophilicity categories based on their interaction with water molecules. Hydrophobic Graphene Nanoplatelets repel water and are commonly used in applications where water resistance is required. On the other hand, Hydrophilicity Graphene Nanoplatelets attract water molecules, making them suitable for applications where water absorption or dispersibility is needed. Both types of Graphene Nanoplatelets offer unique properties and find diverse applications in industries such as electronics, aerospace, and energy.</p></p>
