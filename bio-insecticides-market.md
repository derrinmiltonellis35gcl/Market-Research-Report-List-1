<p><p>Bio-insecticides are derived from natural sources such as bacteria, fungi, and viruses, making them environmentally friendly alternatives to chemical pesticides. Three common types of bio-insecticides include Bacillus Thuringiensis, Beauveria Bassiana, and Metarhizium Anisopliae. Bacillus Thuringiensis produces proteins toxic to certain insect species, while Beauveria Bassiana and Metarhizium Anisopliae are fungi that infect and kill insects. These bio-insecticides are becoming increasingly popular in agriculture due to their effectiveness and minimal impact on non-target organisms.</p></p>
