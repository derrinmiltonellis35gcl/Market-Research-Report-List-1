<p><p>Biopharmaceutical drug delivery devices are classified into different types based on their mode of administration. Injection type devices involve the direct delivery of drugs into the bloodstream through a needle or syringe. Mucosal type devices administer drugs through the mucous membranes, like nasal sprays or inhalers. Implanted type devices are placed inside the body to release drugs gradually over time, such as drug-eluting implants. Other types include transdermal patches and oral drug delivery systems. Each type offers distinct benefits in terms of convenience, efficacy, and targeted drug delivery.</p></p>
